# Case Study 04 – Rubric-Based Evaluation for Child-Friendly Explanations

## Objective

Evaluate two AI-generated responses using a structured scoring rubric to determine which response better satisfies the user's request.

---

## User Prompt

> Explain what a rainbow is in simple language for a child.

---

## Response A

> A rainbow is made when sunlight shines through tiny drops of water in the air. The light bends and separates into different colors, creating a beautiful rainbow in the sky.

---

## Response B

> A rainbow is a meteorological optical phenomenon caused by the refraction, dispersion, and internal reflection of light within water droplets, resulting in a continuous spectrum.

---

# Evaluation Rubric

| Criterion | Response A | Response B |
|-----------|:----------:|:----------:|
| Instruction Following | 5/5 | 2/5 |
| Accuracy | 5/5 | 5/5 |
| Clarity | 5/5 | 2/5 |
| Helpfulness | 5/5 | 2/5 |
| Overall Score | **5/5** | **2/5** |

---

## Better Response

**Response A**

---

## Primary Criterion

**Instruction Following**

---

## Final Justification

Response A fully satisfies the user's request by explaining the concept of a rainbow in simple, child-friendly language while remaining factually accurate. The explanation is clear, easy to understand, and appropriate for the intended audience.

Response B is factually accurate but does not follow the user's instruction to explain the concept in simple language for a child. It uses technical scientific terminology such as "meteorological optical phenomenon," "refraction," and "dispersion," making it difficult for the intended audience to understand.

Therefore, Response A is the better response because it effectively balances accuracy, clarity, and age-appropriate communication.

---

## Key Learning

When evaluating AI-generated responses, accuracy alone is not sufficient. Evaluators must also consider whether the response is appropriate for the intended audience. A technically correct response may still receive a lower score if it fails to match the user's requested reading level, tone, or context.
