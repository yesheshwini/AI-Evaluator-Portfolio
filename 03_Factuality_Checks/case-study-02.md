# Case Study 02 – Detecting Factual Hallucinations

## Objective

Evaluate two AI-generated responses and determine which response is factually accurate while identifying hallucinations.

---

## User Prompt

> Can penguins fly?

---

## Response A

> Penguins cannot fly. They are flightless birds that use their wings to swim underwater.

---

## Response B

> Penguins usually fly only during winter because cold air helps them stay airborne.

---

# Evaluation

## Better Response

**Response A**

## Primary Criterion

**Accuracy (Truthfulness)**

## Hallucination

**Yes**

## Severity

**Major**

## Justification

Response A accurately states that penguins are flightless birds and explains how they use their wings for swimming. Response B presents a false claim that penguins can fly during winter, which is a clear factual hallucination. Since the prompt asks for factual information, accuracy is the most important evaluation criterion. Therefore, Response A better satisfies the user's request.

---

## Key Learning

A factual hallucination occurs when an AI confidently presents false information as true. Evaluators should identify incorrect claims, assess their severity, and explain why the accurate response better serves the user's intent.
